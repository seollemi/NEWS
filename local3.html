NBI tasked to investigate 'illegal activities' of foreign diplomats

Adrian Ayalin, ABS-CBN News
Published May 14, 2024 05:20 PM PHT

MANILA — The Department of Justice (DOJ) has ordered the National Bureau of Investigation (NBI) to conduct an in-depth investigation on foreign diplomats committing illegal and unlawful activities in the country.

In a statement, Justice Secretary Jesus Crispin Remulla said diplomatic immunity is not absolute.

The DOJ did not refer to diplomats of specific countries in its statement but it noted reports on the Chinese embassy releasing alleged transcripts and recordings of a conversation that supposedly happened between a Chinese diplomat and a Philippine military official involving Ayungin Shoal.

“While enjoying the privileges and immunities accorded to foreign diplomats, it is also their duty to respect the laws and regulations of the receiving state,” Remulla said in the statement.

The DOJ also said appropriate actions, if warranted, will be taken against the concerned diplomats.

The DOJ noted that the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations states that diplomats, employees of international organizations and their immediate family members are covered by a certain degree of diplomatic immunity during their stay in a receiving State.

But the DOJ added their diplomatic immunity is only with respect to official acts performed in the exercise of official duties and functions necessary for advocating international comity, policies and interests.

“Diplomatic immunity should never be used as a license to exploit our country’s peace and harmony for selfish motives, this privilege does not shield anyone from the consequences of the Rule of Law,” Remulla said.
